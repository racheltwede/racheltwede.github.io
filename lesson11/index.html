Welcome to The Weather Corner, the official community website for Preston, Soda Springs, and Fish Haven—three towns tucked into Idaho's beautiful southeastern corner. Here, you'll find information on the weather and local events for these towns, as well articles relevant to each location. The site also offers a page for reporting large storms or other weather events and a photo gallery showcasing some of the different weather conditions in the area.

Record Temperatures to Hit Southern Idaho this Summer

Article by Abby Johnson

If you thought last year's summer was hot, then it looks like we have some bad news. Not only will the next months likely continue our long wave of abnormally hot weather, but some experts say that we're likely to see higher temperatures than have ever been recorded in our community. Our current predictions say that we might be seeing temperatures of up to a whopping 106°F this July—a full five degrees higher than the current record! This heat wave isn't letting up, and it's about to get a whole lot worse before it gets better.

As someone whose family has lived in or near Franklin County for generations, I've heard all sorts of stories about unusual storms and weather events. As a kid, I would often wonder what it would be like to live through a record-breaking snowfall or to see one of the rare tornadoes that occassionally passes through the area. Now that it looks like I'm about to watch a new weather record be made, though, I have to admit that it's not all it's cracked up to be.

More worrisome than the discomfort of feeling a little warm is the increase risk for drought and wildfires. The fact that we had a dry, mostly snow-free winter doesn't exactly help us on that front. Water levels in local resevoirs may not be at so-called "emergency levels" yet, but I wouldn't be surprised to see restrictions on water usage being mandated sometime soon.
